Bison Stew

Bison stew is a hearty and flavorful dish that combines tender chunks of bison meat with a medley of vegetables and rich, savory broth. Known for its lean and slightly sweet flavor, bison is a fantastic alternative to beef and provides a nutritious, protein-packed meal. This stew is perfect for cozy dinners or meal preps, offering warmth and satisfaction in every bite.

Slow-cooked to perfection, the stew allows the bison meat to become incredibly tender while soaking up the flavors of aromatic herbs, spices, and the natural sweetness of root vegetables. It's a comforting dish that captures the essence of rustic cooking, ideal for family gatherings or as a standalone meal during colder months.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (900 g) bison stew meat, cut into bite-sized chunks
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 medium carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 3 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 1 cup (240 ml) diced tomatoes (canned or fresh)
  • 4 cups (950 ml) beef or bison broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons cold water (optional, for thickening)

Steps to Make Bison Stew

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Cut the bison meat into bite-sized pieces and season with salt and pepper. Chop all the vegetables and set them aside.

  2. Brown the Meat: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the bison meat and sear on all sides until browned. Remove the meat and set it aside.

  3. Sauté Aromatics: In the same pot, add diced onions and minced garlic. Sauté for 2–3 minutes until softened and fragrant.

  4. Add Vegetables and Tomato Paste: Stir in the carrots, potatoes, and celery. Cook for about 5 minutes, then add the tomato paste and cook for another minute to enhance the flavor.

  5. Combine Liquids and Herbs: Return the browned bison meat to the pot. Add diced tomatoes, beef or bison broth, thyme, rosemary, and bay leaves. Bring the mixture to a boil.

  6. Simmer the Stew: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally. This allows the meat to become tender and the flavors to meld.

  7. Adjust Thickness: If desired, mix cornstarch with cold water and stir it into the stew. Let it cook for another 5 minutes until thickened.

  8. Season and Serve: Taste the stew and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Serve hot with crusty bread or over a bed of rice. Enjoy!
