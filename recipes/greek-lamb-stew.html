Greek Lamb Stew

Greek lamb stew is a delicious Mediterranean dish that combines tender pieces of lamb with a blend of vegetables, herbs, and a rich tomato-based sauce. Infused with traditional Greek flavors like garlic, oregano, and cinnamon, this stew is hearty and aromatic, making it a perfect choice for comforting family dinners or special gatherings.

Slow-cooked to perfection, the lamb becomes succulent and tender while the vegetables absorb the savory flavors of the broth. Paired with crusty bread, rice, or orzo, Greek lamb stew delivers a warm, satisfying meal that is as flavorful as it is nourishing.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (900 g) lamb shoulder or leg, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 medium carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup (240 ml) canned diced tomatoes
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 cup (240 ml) red wine (optional)
  • 3 cups (720 ml) chicken or beef broth
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons cold water (optional, for thickening)

  1. Prepare the Ingredients: Cut the lamb into bite-sized pieces and season with salt and pepper. Chop all the vegetables and set them aside.

  2. Brown the Meat: In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the lamb pieces and sear on all sides until browned. Remove the meat and set it aside.

  3. Sauté Aromatics: In the same pot, add diced onions and minced garlic. Sauté for 2–3 minutes until softened and fragrant.

  4. Add Vegetables and Tomato Paste: Stir in the carrots and potatoes. Cook for 5 minutes, then add the tomato paste and cook for another minute to enhance the flavor.

  5. Combine Liquids and Herbs: Return the browned lamb to the pot. Add diced tomatoes, red wine (if using), broth, oregano, cinnamon, and bay leaf. Stir to combine and bring the mixture to a boil.

  6. Simmer the Stew: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally. The lamb should become tender, and the flavors should meld beautifully.

  7. Adjust Thickness: If desired, mix cornstarch with cold water and stir it into the stew. Let it cook for another 5 minutes until thickened.

  8. Season and Serve: Taste the stew and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot with crusty bread, rice, or orzo. Enjoy!
